What are Shri Gurudev's views on Bhakti?
Gurudev Sangrah

Here's what the corpus contains on this:

“Mysticism denotes that attitude of mind which involves a direct, immediate, intuitive apprehension of God. It signifies the highest attitude of which man is capable, namely, a beatific contemplation of God, and its dissemination in the Society and the World.”

— Pathway to God in Hindi Literature, General Introduction (canonical) Shri Gurudev Ranade

“Kabir surpasses other Hindi saints in his description of the spiritual Teacher with his moral, mystical and social qualities. He has laid great stress on the importance of the Name of God.”

— Pathway to God in Hindi Literature, Summary of Kabir (canonical) Shri Gurudev Ranade

These passages frame bhakti as both the intuitive apprehension of God and the path practiced by the great saints — in Kabir's case, through the constant remembrance of the Name.

Pravachan Outline

Thesis

Adhyay 12 of the Bhagavad Gita establishes bhakti as the most accessible and the most exalted of yogas. Shri Gurudev Ranade's own life at Nimbal embodied this teaching — quiet, continuous remembrance of the guru-mantra as the ground of every action.

Canonical anchor

“Bhakti is the very air that the mystic breathes; it is not one yoga among many, but the soul of them all.”

— Bhagavad Gita as a Philosophy of God-Realisation, ch. 12 (canonical) Shri Gurudev Ranade
Supporting athvani
  • The trunks of Bhausaheb Maharaj's letters

    “Gurudev preserved Bhausaheb Maharaj's correspondence in wooden trunks at the Nimbal house. Every morning he would wash his hands before opening them — we never saw him touch the letters otherwise.”

    — निंबाळचे जुने घर, “जैसी गंगा वाहे” collection (athvani) narrated by Vijaya Apte
  • Allahabad mornings

    “Even in his university years at Allahabad, he would be seated in namasadhana long before dawn. For him bhakti was discipline, not mood.”

    — Allahabad days (athvani) recorded by V.H. Date
  • The Dharwad library donation

    “He gave the whole library to the university with no announcement, no ceremony. He treated it as an offering, not a transaction.”

    — Ranade chronology, Dharwad entry (reference) Gurudev Ranade bibliography
Suggested sequence

Open with the canonical anchor to ground listeners in Adhyay 12. Move into the three athvani in chronological order — Nimbal, Allahabad, Dharwad — tracing bhakti from inner discipline to outward offering. Close on the Dharwad gesture as the fruition.

Pathway to God in Hindi Literature

Shri Gurudev Ranade · General Introduction (excerpt)

Page 4 of 18
¶ 1 Mysticism denotes that attitude of mind which involves a direct, immediate, intuitive apprehension of God. It signifies the highest attitude of which man is capable, namely, a beatific contemplation of God, and its dissemination in the Society and the World. The surest criterion of mysticism is the reality of the experience as enjoyed by the mystic himself.
¶ 2 It is known only to him and to his Maker. Before it there is no appeal; for it there is no criterion. It is this personal-divine aspect of a mystic's spiritual realisation which stamps it with a peculiar halo and worth.
¶ 3 It is in this sense that mystical experience has been regarded as ineffable. It has been very often supposed that, for mystical experience, no separate faculty like intuition need be requisitioned, but that intellect, feeling, and will might suffice to enable us to have a full experience of God.

Design context

All seven mockups use the warm-devotional aesthetic per ADR-006: parchment background, sepia text, maroon accent, warm serif fonts (Lora + Noto Serif Devanagari companion when it loads). System serifs are used as fallback so the feel is approximate but representative. Q&A and Pravachan answers follow the quote-first pattern per ADR-007: verbatim passages with attribution directly below, no inline citation markers, no bottom accordion. Screen patterns follow the amended RFC-004.

These are static visual mockups — the only interactivity is hover states (mode dropdown, share options, ghost buttons) to show how affordances respond. No JavaScript.
