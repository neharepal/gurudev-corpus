Citation Panel — three layouts

Same question, same answer, same three citations — only the layout differs. All three use the "warm devotional" aesthetic (ADR-006): parchment background, sepia text, maroon accent, warm serif fonts. Compare how each feels, not just how each looks.

Layout 1

Right-side pinned panel

Pros: All sources always visible. Easy comparison. Feels scholarly.
Cons: Less reading width for the answer. Collapses to bottom on mobile.
What are Shri Gurudev's views on Bhakti?

For Shri Gurudev Ranade, bhakti is not a sentimental devotion but the deepest and most direct path to God-realization#1. He places it at the apex of the four classical yogas, arguing that jnana, karma, and dhyana all culminate in bhakti when fully realized#1.

In his survey of mystical traditions, Gurudev draws particular attention to the Marathi sant tradition — Jnaneshwar, Tukaram, Eknath, Ramdas — as embodiments of the bhakti path in lived form#2.

The corpus also preserves recollections that connect Gurudev's writings to his own practice: devotees describe how his daily life at Nimbal was itself an expression of unwavering bhakti toward his own guru, Shri Bhausaheb Maharaj#3.
