सत्संग पेज नंबर ७

कव्हर नसल्याने कोणता महिना सत्संग हे समजत नाही.

मुखपृष्ठावर सत्संग विषयी थोडेसे प्रकट चिंतन असे लिहिले आहे.

All Faiths' Conference: Gurudev's Message

Shri Swami Banthanal Maharaj deserves to be heartily congratulated on his very happy idea of holding an 'All Faiths' Conference' to bring together men of all kinds of religious belief, and creed on the same intellectual platform with a view to bringing about a real understanding, harmony and peace between communities in India in general and in Karnataka in particular. For, it is only the spiritual unity of all communities and creeds that is of an abiding and permanent nature, while all groups based on material principles are but superficial and short-lived, as they are not sufficiently broad-based and self-less in their nature.

श्री स्वामी बंथनाळ महाराज यांनी लच्चाण येथे 'All Faiths' Conference' सर्व धर्म परिषद भरवली होती. श्री गुरुदेवांनी त्यांचे अभिनंदन केले आहे. वेगवेगळ्या धर्माचे व वेगवेगळ्या श्रद्धा असलेले लोक एकाच बौद्धिक पातळीवर आणून त्यांच्यात खऱ्या अर्थाने एकी व शांती प्रस्थापित करण्यासाठी त्यांनी ही परिषद भरवली होती. कारण अध्यात्मिक नातेच शेवटी अतूट आणि कायम स्वरूपाचे असते आणि व्यवहारिक नाते वरवरचे व थोड्या काळापुरते टिकणारे असते कारण त्यामध्ये दृष्टिकोन विशाल नसतो व स्वार्थ असतो.

Such latter trends of thought deserve to be corrected by a broad and fundamentally unifying principle. And this we cannot find anywhere else except in the spiritual nature of man.

Men are generally misled by false ideologies and values in life. These values have to be transvalued in the light of what would be permanently useful and beneficial to the whole humanity without any distinction of caste, creed or nationality. It is only by realizing the essential unity of human nature and thereby establishing the fatherhood of God and the brotherhood of man that the real good of humanity can be achieved. This is the ideal preached and practiced by all saints, sages and prophets of the world in the past. Spiritual Religion, the creed of all saints of the world, can alone save us from all separatists, disorganising and dissipating tendencies and forces. It is from this viewpoint that the efforts of Shri Banthanal Swamiji to hold an 'All Faiths' Conference' at Lachyan deserve to be commended heartily to all good and God-loving people. He had disinterestedly helped the foundation of an institution for higher education in Karnatak. His intellectual ability and moral enthusiasm can be judged from his influence on his disciples. The tree as the Biblical expression goes, is justified by its fruits. I was thus greatly impressed by the ideals and manners of two of his disciples, with whom I came into contact recently. Shri Swamiji has also been fortunate in securing Shri Gauri Shankar Swamiji, the head of Gauri Math, Mysore, as the President of the Conference. Swami Gauri Shankarji is well-versed in all the six Shastras and is a distinguished M.A. from Nadia. I am greatly delighted to learn that such a great scholar of wide experience and knowledge is to guide and direct the deliberations and the proceedings of the Conference. I am also exceedingly glad to find tha great scholars of various faiths, from far and near, have been invited to discourse on various religions and their special features. I wish their deliberations every success. This assembly of distinguished scholars, pandits and spiritual aspirants reminds me of the grand congregation of the great saints that had assembled in Kalyanpattana under the presidentship of Shri Prabhudev. He was popularly known as Allama Prabhu and was one of the greatest saints of all times. A graphic description of the assembly at Basav-Kalyan has been given in the spiritual song, इदे अनुभव मंटपवु which has filled me with delight for many years past. I heard it for the first time from Shri Nagappa, the grandson of the Saint of Nimbargi who has been one of the greatest saints of modern India. It was the life-task of this great Saint to practice and preach the essential nature of the mystical religion throughout his long life, extending almost over a century. As an humble disciple of the disciple of this great saint, I feel assured, as Shakespeare has said, that like the pole star in the heavens he will spread his beneficial influence all over Karnataka. To put it in the words of a Sanskrit poet, like the Bakula tree planted in the corner of the garden, he will fill the whole atmosphere with the fragrance of spiritual experience. joy and peace. I heartily recommend to this congregation who may be thirsting for spiritual life to understand the real nature of the Laya or God absorption about which the poem,, is speaking, and call upon Shri Nagappa to sing the song to this Conference. I wish the Conference every success.

- Copy of the message sent by Prof. Ranade to for held at Lachyan on 29-04-1948.
