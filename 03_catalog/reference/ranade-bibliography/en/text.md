Chronological Order of Writings of Shri Gurudeo:-

*Nityanemavali- in 1911.
(This is the first great little book of daily prayer was prepared under the guidence of Shri
Bhausaheb Maharaj in Marathi by Shri Gurudev.It is primarily a book of selections ,specially
designed for the disciples of Nimbaragi - sampraday. The introduction contains the seeds of all
the outstanding ideas that can be found in a fully develoed form in all the major works of Shri
Gurudev. It is the epitome of his future works.Shri Maharaj remarked," Just see! How he has
done everything and yet has chosen to conceal his identity!")

*Bodhsudha-in 1948.
(Ambrosia of Advice. It is,his first Kannada book which is prepared with the help of some his
diciples.He got a detailed introduction written to it,in Marathi,under his own directions and
published in Devnagari script in 1948.This book contains the sayings of Shri Nimbaragi
Maharaj,the greatest master of his master,the virtual originator of his sampraday,Nimbaragi
Sampraday.It deals primarily with ethics and mysticism and divided into four
parts:a)Fundamental Principles of Conduct b) Renunciation of Vices c) Cultivation of Virtues and
d)Pathway to God.)

1.A critical edition of Carlyle's - Characteristics and Signs of the Times- in 1916/in 1914.
(It is ,Shri Gurudeo Ranade's only work on literature and was written when he was teaching
English in Fergusson College,Pune.It gives a foretaste of his writings,all of which display a
significant trait of his mind - the trait of being in search of unity.
This,Signs Of Times and Characteristics by Thomas Carlyle- in An Eldorado of literary
information,the book is an edition by Shri Gurudeo Ranade,of Carlyle's two essays.This is the

The Educational Review of Madras wrote about it in these superlative terms:
"Without overshooting the mark, we may say confidently that no annotation of any English
classic by an Indian has reached the leve of excellence attained by Mr. Rānade in the present
work."

2.A Constructive Survey of Upanishadic Philosophy - in 1926.
(A positive approach to the profound doctrines contained in thr Upanishadas.This book was
planned to be 10th of Sixteen - Volume scheme of writing an encyclopaedic History of Indian
Philosophy envisaged to cover,'the three chief departments of thought,namely,European,Indian
Philosophy and Philosophy of Religion.The work is based on the lectures deliverd at Bangalore
and Mysore at the time of the foundation of the Sanskrit Academy in Bangalore in July 1915.The
book at once reveals the philosophical genius in Gurudev Ranade and drew the attention of
scholars the world over.R. Zimmermann exclaimed: 'A mere glance at the book will show how
far you have gone beyond Deussen's Philosophy of the Upanishads. ' One has just to remember
that Deussen's work was till then the only avenue of reaching the philosophy of the
Upanishads.)

8.The Mysticism in Maharashtra - in 1933.

So far the chonology of publications of books of Shri Gurudeo.With another basis of
classification we can say that while A Constructive Survey of Upnishadic Philosophy is synthetic
in its approach,the Creative Period is analytic. The former treats the subject topic-wise while the
latter Upanişad-wise. So too, Mysticism in Maharashtra is analytic in its approach-it takes up the
saints one by one and gives us their religious philosophy; while Pathway to God in Hindi
Literature and Pathway to God in Kannada Literature are synthetic, we have a framework of the
subject and the saints come and go incidentally. The thought is more important than the
individuals. The analytic approach is descriptive while the synthetic one is interpretative. It was
his great desire to re-cast Mysticism in Maharashtra in the synthetic mould. He had expressed
this desire in a lecture on Karnatak Mysticism delivered on 26th August 1950 at Dharwar. The
task, however, could not be taken up.

C.Outward Letters-
The following lines from a letter to an ailing friend show how his spiritual advice was most direct
and simple. * You can remember the नाम as and when it suits you. Better to get yourself
accustomed to माळ. You can complete it with ease, have some rest and commence again. Do
not put yourself to the strain of too much thinking: do not allow your doubts and unnecessary
reasoning to disturb your balance of mind. Have faith and increase Bhaktis ". Most of his letters
end with sentences as Kindly let me know how your meditation is progressing " Spiritual practice
constitutes the perorative refrain of all his letters. In a letter to a friend who was bereaved he
writes : "The highest loyalty that you have all along been cherishing the Great Ideal of spiritual
enlightenment must be, I think, the real source of relief and peace of mind to you. It is only
under the soothing influence of Divine Grace that man learns to forget the ills and misfortunes
that this earthly life is heir to". It is made clear that spiritual life alone is a panacea for all
maladies.

Characteristics of His Writings:-
  1)Silence about Himself-
especially true of earlier in A Constructive Survey of Upanishadic Philosophy and Mysticism in
Maharashtra,the writer is fully in the background.
In later,Pathway to God In Hindi Mysticism and more so in Bhagvadgita as a Philosophy of God
Realisation,and much more in Kannad Mysticism,Shri Gurudev alludes to many an incident in
his life and allowed himself to be very free with the readers,which makes reading very lively.In
fact ,a personal touch made itself felt from the days when the Dhyan -Gita was under
preparation in the early forties.
  Many times he refers the teachings of Shri Bhausaheb Maharaj ( his own spiritual teacher )
and Shri Nimbargi Maharaj ( the spiritual teacher of Shri Bhausaheb Maharaj ). Apart from the
spiritual and philosophical interest in their teachings, there can also be present in Gurudev's
mind a desire to preserve living portraits of the twɔ great giants in the spiritual line to which he
belonged. Mostly he has not put his own views independently. He speaks through the mouths of
others and remains hidden. It was his humility to think that when great saints have given so
much to the world,why should he speak of his own experiences? The systematisation displayed
in culling out sources is the genesis of his method of construction through critical exposition
2) Constructive Criticism-
His approach to philosophical doctrines was that of reconciliation.
3)Comprehensiveness and Internal Systematization.
4)Asymtotic Approximation-
The curve of hyperbola never meets the asymptote; it goes on approaching it continuously
without actually meeting it or meeting it at infinity. This is the conception of asymptotic
approximation. Similarly the devotee on his spiritual pathway goes on coming closer and closer
to God as he pursues the path, and meets God at infinity.
All ends are accomplished gradually, bit by bit. This is सोपान-आरोहण न्याय or climbing a staircase
step by step. What Psychology calls illumination or a sudden understanding of a problem,
comes only after the period of preparation and incubation. This characteristic of progressive
realisation is applied to morality and mysticism.
5)Reciprocal Causality-
"As you become more and more sinless,you get more and more of the vision of God, and as
you get more and more of vision of God,you become more and more sinless.Becoming sinless
is a gradual process,having a vision of God is also a gradual process and they are causes and
effects of each other.
6)Spiritual Purpose-
God is the central thread running through the pearls of Gurudev's thought and it holds together
diverse subjects and diverse interests.
7)Style-
His style was simple,that is,his meaning could be understood with ease.And once understood,it
would take permanent hold of the memory.
