## LLM answer

The Nimbargi Sampraday (also called the Nimbaragi Sampraday) is a spiritual lineage rooted in the teachings of Shri Nimbargi Maharaj (1789–1885), a saint who lived at Devar Nimbargi in Karnataka. It is a "Swarup Sampraday" — a tradition completely founded on the principles of Bhakti (devotional worship) and Guru, in which God-realization is reached through Self-realization or Self-Vision. The central teaching of Shri Nimbargi Maharaj is that one must dedicate one's worldly life to the Self and remain engaged in meditation on the Name of God — and crucially, this can be done while living an ordinary family life, without renouncing the world. The lineage passed from Nimbargi Maharaj through Bhausaheb Maharaj and Amburao Maharaj to Shri Gurudev R. D. Ranade, who became its most prolific expositor, authoring works in English, Marathi, Kannada, Hindi, and Sanskrit. Gurudev's first major publication, the Nityanemavali (1911), was prepared under the guidance of Bhausaheb Maharaj and was "specially designed for the disciples of Nimbaragi Sampraday." Nimbargi Maharaj himself composed around 19 devotional songs (18 in Kannada, 1 in Hindi), and his ethical and mystical teachings were compiled by Gurudev in the Bodhsudha (1948).
