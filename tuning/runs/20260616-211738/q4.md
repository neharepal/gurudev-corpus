## LLM answer

You're looking at **Pathway to God in Hindi Literature** by R. D. Ranade, published by the Shri Gurudev Ranade Samadhi Trust, Nimbal.

The General Introduction gives a lovely orientation to what the book is about:

> "It has been a long-cherished aim of the present writer to gather the multi-coloured flowers of the mystic garden, and to present a garland of them to the Lord."

The book is organized around **five broad themes** common to all spiritual seekers. As Ranade states in Chapter I:

> "First, there are certain incentives which prompt men to spiritual life — philosophical, axiological and physiological. Then, there is the question of moral and spiritual preparation... Thirdly, they have to keep before themselves certain exemplars of conduct and attainment... the nature and functions of God... Finally... they can envisage to themselves certain landmarks, certain lamp-posts, which will help them to walk with courage and confidence on the pathway to God."

The Publishers' Note also recommends reading the companion volume **परमार्थसोपान** alongside this one, as it contains the original Padas and Dohas that Ranade discusses here. A good place to begin is **Part One, Chapter I: Incentives of Spiritual Life**.
