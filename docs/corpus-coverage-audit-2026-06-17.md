1. **All of V.H. Date's writings** (25 titles, 0%). Date was Gurudev's senior disciple and the most prolific English-language expositor of the Nimbal lineage. Without his corpus, the English-side coverage of the lineage's philosophy is thin.
2. **All letters** (10 titles, 0%). Direct counsel from Gurudev / Bhausaheb / Amburao to named individuals. Unique register — neither doctrinal-treatise nor athvani.
3. **All of Ramanna Kulkarni's writings** (14 titles, 0%). Major Marathi expositor on Ambural Maharaj, Bhausaheb, Namasadhana philosophy.
4. **Most devotee athvani anthologies** (24+ of 29, ~83% missing). Each is a unique anecdotal record from one or two narrators — non-substitutable.
5. **All Bhausaheb-specific works** (5 titles, 0%). The 1959 letters compilation, the biography, the शोध निबंध — Bhausaheb's voice currently reaches the corpus mainly via Patankar Pravachan 3 and Maharajachi Sutre.
6. **Most Gurudev biographies** (16 of 17, 0–6%). The corpus has athvani-style biographical material but none of the formal full biographies.
7. **Scholarly studies** (Sangoram / Padma / B.R. Kulkarni, 5 titles, 0%) — Padma Kulkarni's *Ranade as a Mystic*, B.R. Kulkarni's annotated editions.
