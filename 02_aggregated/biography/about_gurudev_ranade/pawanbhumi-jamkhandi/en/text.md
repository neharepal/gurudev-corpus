## Shree Gurudeo Ranade Paramartha Mandir

**"Pavitra Te Kul, Pawan To Desh.**
**Jethe Hariche Das, Janma Gheti"- Saint Tukaram Maharaj**

(Revered) Param Poojya Shree Nimbargi Maharaj, Param Poojya Shree Bhau Saheb Maharaj, Param Poojya Shree Amburao Maharaj - the great Realised (Atmasakshatkari) Saints of Nimbargi Sampradaya were visiting Jamkhandi often. As a result Jamkhandi became Holy place where on 3rd July 1886, Rev. Shree Gurudeo Ranade, Godly man (Dev Manav) was born, who in future became a renowned Philosopher and Realised (Atmasakshatkari) Saint. There is no wonder that Rev. Gurudeo's Birth-place and residential house has thus turned into 'Paramarth Mandir'. Above is the photograph of this Mandir.

With the intention of providing preliminary information to the visitors of 'Paramartha Mandir' - especially new visitors-we were thinking for a very long time, of publishing a booklet. We are very glad to announce that this idea has come in to reality. Informative Booklet is being published **on the auspicious occasion of Rev. Shree Gurudeo's 125th Birthday grand celebrations** and Naam-saptah of five days.

With the intention of benefit to maximum people, we have given the essential information in 3 languages viz. English, Marathi, kannada.

We are offering this as our small service at the Holy Feet of the four Rev. Saints of Nimbargi Sampradaya.

## RAMTIRTH MANDIR
## रामतीर्थ मंदीर

Famous Lord Shankar Mandir which is located on the hillock in Jamkhandi. Near to this temple is the palace of the rulers of the erstwhile Jamkhandi state. During the birth of Rev. Gurudeo, his mother could not bear the pangs of labour. All the family members were worried. They were thinking of taking the mother to hospital for surgical aid. Just then the following remedy was used by Krishnappa an old servant in the house.

Srimant Ramchandrarao Appasaheb Patwardhan, the then ruling chief of Jamkhandi was a pious devotee of a spotless character. Many a mother had been relieved from the pangs of labour by drinking the Tirth (sacred water). During the birth of Rev. Gurudeo, his mother could not get the pangs of labour. All the family members were worried. They were thinking of taking the mother to hospital for surgical aid. Just then the following remedy was used by Krishnappa an old servant in the house. The pious chief got up from his bed, took bath, worshipped Shri Rameshwar and sent sacred water through Krishnappa. Matoshri Parvati drank the holy water and her delivery was smooth and safe. In this way with the grace of Rameshwar Rev. Gurudeo was born at 10 am on 3rd July 1886. He was named Ramachandra at the suggestion of the chief.

Another important incident connected with this Temple is as follows. In 1956, during the Amrut Mahotsava celebrations, Rev. Gurudeo lit camphor in

## MARUTI MANDIR
## मारुती मंदिर

Young Gurudeo had sharp intellect, but he could not stand first in his class for some time. He discovered the reason by minutely observing the daily routine of the boy who secured first place. He found that the boy daily visited Maruti temple and bowed down to the deity. There after young Gurudeo started the same practice. Before going to school, he would begin mental recitation of the 'Hymn to Hanuman' by saint Ramdas, finish it till he reached Maruti's temple on the way, lay prostrate before the deity and then proceed to the school. Within a few months, young Gurudeo stood first in his class. This increased his devotion to the deity.

ಜಮಖಂಡಿ ಇಲ್ಲಿಯ ಪರಶುರಾಮ ಭಾವು ಹೈಸ್ಕೂಲ ಇಲ್ಲಿ ಪ.ಪೂ. ಶ್ರೀ ಗುರುದೇವರು ಮೆಟ್ರಿಕ್ ವರೆಗೆ ವ್ಯಾಸಂಗ ಮಾಡಿದರು. 1966 ರ ಜುಲೈ 3 ರಂದು 80 ನೇ ಜನ್ಮ ದಿನದ ನಿಮಿತ್ತ ಈ ಪ್ರತಿಮೆಯನ್ನು ಸ್ಥಾಪಿಸಲಾಯಿತು. 1997 ರಲ್ಲಿ ಈ ಪ್ರತಿಮೆಯ ಸಂರಕ್ಷಣೆಗೆ ಹಾಗೂ ಶೋಭಾಯಮಾನವಾಗಿ ಕಾಣಲೆಂದು Wrought Iron Grid ಸುಂದರ ಬಣ್ಣ ಗಳಿಂದ ಸಿಂಗರಿಸಲಾಯಿತು.

During **'Amrut Mahotsava'** celebrations at Jamkhandi, in his speech in Kannada, Rev. Shree Gurudeo said, "Jamkhandi is pious because of association of great saints. I am fortunate to have taken birth in Jamkhandi."

"On one occasion, when Prof. Ranade was in a particularly happy mood, he narrated to me his belief in the presence of a spiritual current, which like rain-water is percolated in the soil. This current is found in different volumes & at different depths. Sometimes the spiritual current is near to the surface of the ground. In such a case, any person with high spiritual power, walking over such ground will immediately feel that some force underground is exerting a pull on his feet. Such spots are said to be sacred spots and are selected by saints as places for erecting Ashrams & Temples.Gurudeo said that he had seen three such spots in his life- Allahabad (where he was working as Vice-Chancellor of the Allahabad University), Nimbal and Jamkhandi. He told me that the spiritual current at Allahabad & Nimbal was comparitively more powerful than at Allahabad & Nimbal."

[Extract from the reminiscences of Shri B.D.Jatti (Ex Vice-President of India) from the book 'Gurudeo Ranade (Biography & Mysticism)' by S.N.Deshpande]
