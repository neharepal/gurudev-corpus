Reviews on ‘The Evolution of My Own Thought’

This Article is contributed by Gurudev Ranade in

‘Contemporary Indian Philosophy’

Published by George Allen and Unwin Ltd., London (1936)

71- Prof. Arthur T. Shillinglaw

“Prof. Ranade's sketch of the development of his own thought is, I think, the most original of the contributions. His practice is to reach truth by a critical interpretation of the great philosophers, and he gives a few specimens of his method. These clearly indicate interpretative genius of a very high order, as witness, his cogent criticism of Zeller and Burnet on Parmenides and his comments on the Upanishadic Theories of Self-Consciousness.”

(Extract of a Review from 'Mind' an International Journal on Philosophy, London, Volume 46, Issue 187, July 1937, Pg. 406)

72- Prof. T. G.

“Prof. Ranade’s contribution, in which he proposes to reach truth by a critical interpretation of the great philosophers, is perhaps the most original of the series.”

(Extract from ‘Nature’, a British multidisciplinary Scientific Journal, Vol. 142, Pg. 55, 1938)

73- Prof. Alban G. Widgery

Professor of Philosophy, Duke University, North Carolina

“Perhaps more than any other contributor Prof. Ranade of Allahabad has endeavoured explicitly to compare Indian and Western forms on philosophy. In an essay of ‘The Evolution of my Own Thought’ he indicates briefly diverse sides of his work which manifests his acute logical mind. He has a firm conviction that a correlation between Indian and European philosophy is not only possible but necessary in the interest of the development of philosophy in general. Of all those so far considered, Ranade has appeared to me to be the one capable of philosophic detachment; nevertheless, from the hints he gives it seems he is headed for a position similar to that just indicated.”

(Extract of a Review from the Philosophical Review, New York, Vol. XLVIII, No. I, 1938, Pg. 81)

Prof. A. G. Widgery’s opinion regarding Gurudev Ranade’s writings-

“The difference between Prof. R. D. Ranade’s writings and that of Dr. Radhakrishanan’s is that your (Prof. R. D. Ranade) writing is like that of Prof. A. Ward i.e. short, precise &amp; to the point, there is no repetition of words and that of Dr. Radhakrishnan’s writing was like that of Prof. Bosanquet i.e. big (heavy) words, lots and lots of pages.”
