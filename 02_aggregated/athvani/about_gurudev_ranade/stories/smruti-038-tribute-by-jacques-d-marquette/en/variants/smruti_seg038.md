37- Dr. Jacques D. Marquette

Professor of Philosophy, Paris

The Founder of Scouting in France

“Professor Ranade is one of the most notable thinkers of contemporary India. A distinguished Hellenist,* he held the position of the Chair of Philosophy for a quarter of century and was then the Vice-Chancellor of the University of Allahabad. He was a prolific writer and has remarkable publications to his credit. Some of the prominent ones include: an outstanding essay on Heraclitus whose ideas were considered by the Greek as ‘obscure’; an article on the relation between the Greek thought of the high era and that of India; another important essay on the Spirit of the Upanishads that he wrote for the great History of Indian Philosophy published by the University of Bombay; and a comprehensive volume on the Mystics of Maharashtra. Recently, he has published Pathway to God in Hindi Literature, another valuable contribution that highlights varied aspects of the mystical experiences of great mystics of Central India.

Professor Ranade is not only a profound thinker but has also excelled in bringing to the light the diverse aspects of the Metapsychology. Many Indian philosophers and scholars considered Gurudev as their spiritual instructor and used his advice and support to direct their inner lives. In the spiritual hierarchy of contemporary India, he is the leader of the great jnyanis, yogis and the spiritual aspirants, teaching about the path to divine communion through knowledge and intellectual (Noetic) axiology.”44

(Translated from original French to English)

*means- a specialist in the language or culture of ancient Greece.

“My meeting with this real Mahatma, Prof. Dr. Ranade, the foremost Jnyana Yogi of our age, had an all-penetrating influence. He accepted me as a disciple in 1954. The guidance of this great Gurudev, who had been a spiritual adviser to many prominent lights of India, helped me to adjust my mental processes to the interpretation of the inner transmutations called forth by spiritual experiences. It also enabled me to organize my efforts to appreciate the ‘data’ of mystical relationships with the world of ‘intermedia’; between the fount-head of the Universe and its many diversified expressions.”45
