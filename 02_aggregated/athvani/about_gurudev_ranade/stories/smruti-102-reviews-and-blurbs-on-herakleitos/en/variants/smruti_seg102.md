B-Blurbs and Comments on R. D. Ranade’s writings

Reviews on ‘Herakleitos’*

Published by: Prof. R. D. Ranade (Feb.1916)

*Also spelt as Heraclitus or Heracleitus

1- Shri Aurobindo Ghose

An Indian Philosopher, Yogi, Guru, Poet and Nationalist

Founder- Sri Aurobindo Ashram, Pondicherry

“Professor R. D. Ranade has recently published a small treatise on the philosophy of Heraclitus. It is perhaps too much to hope that it is from a series of essays on philosophers or a history of philosophy by this perfect writer and scholar. At any rate, such a work from such a hand would be a priceless gain. For Professor Ranade possesses, in a superlative degree, the rare gift of easy and yet adequate exposition; but he has more than this, for he can give a fascinating interest to subjects like philology and philosophy, which to the ordinary reader, seem harsh, dry, difficult and repellent. He joins to a luminous clarity, lucidity, and charm of expression an equal luminousness and just clarity of presentation and that perfect manner in both native to the Greek and French language and mind, but rare in the English tongue. In these seventeen pages, he has presented the thought of the old enigmatic Ephesin with a clearness and sufficiency, which leaves us charmed, enlightened and satisfied."

[ Arya: A Philosophical Review Journal, Vol. 3, No. 5  (Dec. 1916) Pg. 316 ]

2- H. R. James

Principal, Presidency College, Calcutta

“I have read your paper with growing interest and respect. You appear to be at home with the original Greek, which is in itself a remarkable attainment in one of your countrymen. It is the best study of Heracleitus I have met with; but I must add that of late years I have not been able to read new books on Greek Philosophy. But if all your studies of Greek Thinkers are to be as good, your History of Greek Philosophy should be….. a remarkable achievement.”

(Calcutta, 1st March 1916)

3- Mr. Francis William Bain

Professor of History and Political Economy

A British writer and Principal, Deccan College, Poona

Fellow of All Souls College, Oxford

Prof. F. W. Bain wrote a letter to Prof. R. D. Ranade on February 18, 1916-

“I am much pleased with your essay on Heracleitus. It is very interesting and extremely acute. And as the field of Greek Philosophy is not much cultivated in India, I think you might do well to take it up.”

“There are not even five persons in India, who will understand your scholarly booklets on Greek Philosophy. I shall leave the whole of my library in India but take with me your valuable booklets on Greek Philosophers.”

“And it is for them only that they are written.”  (Replied Gurudev)

Shri Bhausaheb Maharaj was his (Gurudev Ranade’s) spiritual master and Prof. Bain, his intellectual master.

4- Prof. E. A. Wodehouse

Professor of English and Philosophy

Prof. E. A. Wodehouse wrote a letter to Prof. R. D. Ranade on September 6, 1924-

“Many thanks for your papers on Heracleitus, which is quite a brilliant piece of work. It gave me very great pleasure to read it.”
