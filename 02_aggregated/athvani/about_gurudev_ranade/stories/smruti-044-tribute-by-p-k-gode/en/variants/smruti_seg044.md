43- Dr. Parashuram Krishna Gode

A Well-known Sanskrit and Prakrit Scholar

The first Curator of the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute

“One who possesses Vritta or character is a real Arya. Mere acquisition of wealth or learning cannot entitle a man to be called Arya. My contact with the late Dr. Ranade from 1910 to 1957, a period of 47 years, has brought home to my mind the truth of the above definition of an Arya given in the Mahabharata.

After my Matriculation in 1910 I joined the Fergusson College in the Previous Class, to which Dr. Ranade taught English composition. I caught my first inspiration for systematic study from Dr. Ranade at this time. I still remember one of the illustrations of a balanced sentence he gave us from Bain’s Rhetoric, viz. “Books help, when friends do not, and when books do not, meditation”

In 1915, I was his student in B.A. class and came into closer personal contact with him. Dr. Ranade was a man of ideas and ideals. The idea of founding the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute of Poona was first conceived and discussed by Dr. Kurtkoti, Dr. Ranade, Dr. S. K. Belvalkar and Dr. P. D. Gune in 1915, when Dr. Ranade was working as Professor of Philosophy in the Fergusson College, Poona. Dr. Ranade was a member of the Working Committee and a member of the first Executive Board of the Institute.

Dr. Ranade had a brilliant academy career which was crowned. However, after 1909, his health was much shattered by a serious illness from which he partially recovered around 1912. It is really a mystery for us how such ‘a bundle of bones’ could produce such marvellous literary work of a high order. ‘Things of the mind are not of the clay’ said an English poet and the literary output of Dr. Ranade has already become immortal as it has the enduring stamp of his spiritual life.”55

55- The Poona Orientalist, Vol 12, No. 3 (Oct. 1957) Pg. 1-3
