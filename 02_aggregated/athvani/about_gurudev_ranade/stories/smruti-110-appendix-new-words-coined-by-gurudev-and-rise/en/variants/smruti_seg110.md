4- New words coined by Gurudev Ranade

Whenever the current word did not suit his purpose Gurudev Ranade used to coin new words. Thus for the particle of divine light or Bindu. The word ‘Spiriton’ was coined. (It is called Bindu or Bindule. Spiriton was coined at his presidential address at the Philosophical Congress at Nagpur in 1937)

Theonem word was coined by him on the analogy of hominem, populen and verecundium. It is an appeal to the power and actions of God Himself.

Beatificism word was coined by Gurudev Ranade. Beatificism is identification of bliss with ultimate reality. In explaining the ethical doctrine of the Bhagvad-Gita, he has discussed three theories: a general theory of moralism, theory of supermoralism and the theory of beatificism as a reconciliation of the first two. Prof. E. A. Wodehouse was greatly pleased with his coining the word ‘beatificism’.

His explanation of the word Sonnanism is – ‘Sphotism would be a good word; but one might say Sonnanism would be even better to signify the idea conveyed by Kabir in his philosophy of the Spiritual Sound.’ So the word Sonnanism was coined.

He uses the word Theopolity to mean a kingdom where God is the ruler. This was a very favourite idea with Gurudev Ranade.

He coined the word Theotropism during at the time of meditation. Mere tropism might be psychological, mental or imaginary. But theotropism must be a mystical experience.

Spiritual Agniology- A science of agnosticism is Spiritual Agniology.

RISE OF INDIA

“If India must rise, she will rise in a most peculiar way, not hitherto known to all History. She will combine the virtues of the West and the East, and will rise superior to both. If the West and the East are to meet, they will meet in India and not in Europe. What a glorious prospect lies before India! I see India flinging away superstition, sloth and intellectual inertia. I see her taking up the scientific spirit and energy of Europe. I see her assimilating the excellences of both the East and the West and rising in the scale of modern nations, preserving all the while the integrity and the pristine purity of her spiritual self.”
