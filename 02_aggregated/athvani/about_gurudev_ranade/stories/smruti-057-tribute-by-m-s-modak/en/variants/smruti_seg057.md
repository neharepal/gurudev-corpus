56- Dr. M. S. Modak

The Registrar, Nagpur University

“While teaching Plato’s philosophy, he rapidly used to write on the blackboard quotations in the original Greek and explain the thought contained in them, in an excellent manner. That easily enabled the students to understand the special features of Greek philosophers. Once, when he was teaching the standard work of Sidgwick, viz. ‘History of Ethics’, he got a whole paragraph in it deleted by students. ‘Strike of this paragraph from your book,’ he peremptorily ordered, ‘Sidgwick is wrong here.’ It is impossible for the students in the class to forget this spirited utterance of his. He used to speak with haste, as his thought current had a high speed. Hence, to reap the full benefit of his lecture, completely concentrated attention was absolutely necessary. His students attended his lectures with one-pointed rapt attention, on account of a wide awareness that they were in the presence of a highly learned professor…..

Prof. Rambhau was not a mere professor of philosophy. His students could readily realize that he was a great man of realisation, who had found the path for the realisation of Reality and had merged in its meditation.”70

70- Tarun Bharat, Nagpur. 7th July 1956
