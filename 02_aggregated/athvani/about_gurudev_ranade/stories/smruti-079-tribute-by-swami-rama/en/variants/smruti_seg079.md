78- Swami Rama

Founder of Himalayan International Institute of Yoga Science &amp; Philosophy, Pennsylvania, USA

“I often visited Dr. Ranade of Allahabad University, one of the finest exponents of Vedanta Philosophy of his time. This matchless teacher and great mystic was popularly known as Gurudev among his disciples. He took me to his Nimbal Ashram later on. Among all the university scholars of India, I have the most respect for this great man. Whatever I have systemically learned about Indian philosophy was because of him. He taught me that Indian philosophy is divided into seven systems which attempt to answer the most basic philosophical inquiries.”95

“Professor Ranade was unmatched as a scholar of every school and every aspect of Indian Philosophy, but he was most honoured for his profound spiritual wisdom. He was the rare combination of mystic and academician. He was a living example of how to live in the world and yet remain above. Ranade was always busy writings books, giving lectures at the University of Allahabad and other universities, and over seeing his ashram, but despite all this activity I saw in him a mystic who transcended the realm of intellect. Ranade possessed a startling clairvoyance. With the help of Prof. Ranade, I remained aware of the higher goal of life despite all the attractions and distractions the world had to offer. By watching this great man, I was finally convinced that it is possible to be both a scholar and mystic to be successful in the world and yet maintain a spiritual life.”96

95- Swami Rama: Living with the Himalayan Masters (1978) Pg. 251

96- Pandit Rajmani Tigunait: At the Eleventh Hour (2001) Pg. 8
