15- Padma Shri Shrimant Balasaheb Pant Pratinidhi

9th Raja of Aundh State

Shri Gurudev R. D.  Ranade wrote reminiscences of Shrimant Balasaheb-

“I distinctly remember the day when I first saw Shrimant Balasaheb at the Deccan College, on the 23rd March 1909.

In the year 1917, I was invited by the Chiefsaheb for the inaugural meeting which was held in his bungalow in the Narayan Peth, Poona, for the formation of a Committee for the Mahabharata.

In 1919, I met with the Chiefsaheb and Rajasaheb was at the time of the Oriental conference in Poona.  My next recollection goes to the time when I was transferred from the Fergusson College to the Willingdon College, Sangli. To my surprise I found a kindly letter awaiting me from Shrimant Balasaheb before I actually joined, probably as I thought he had welcomed me to the Southern Maratha Country proper. As another Chiefsaheb said to me at the time, “Blood was thicker than water”.  I felt very grateful to them for their kindly manner, and the high ideas which they had cherished about me.

I distinctly remember to have spoken on a theme which was very much after the heart of the Chiefsaheb- “The Making of Great Men.” Ever since then, I have delivered a number of lectures from time to time at Aundh.”18

18- Shri Bhavanrao Gaurav Granth, Part 1 (1934)  Pg. 29
