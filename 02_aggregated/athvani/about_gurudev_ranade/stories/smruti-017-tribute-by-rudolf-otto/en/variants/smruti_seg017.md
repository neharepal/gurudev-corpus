16- Dr. Rudolf Otto

An Eminent German Lutheran Theologian, Philosopher and Comparative Religionist

Gurudev had a great regard for Dr. Rudolf Otto. He says, “Dr. Otto is a good Christian theologian, has a very wide heart and great sympathy with Indian aspirations, and does not find any ultimate distinction between Indian philosophy and European philosophy. Such people we want today in this world; people who would understand the basis of both Christianity and Hinduism. Otto is one of them.”

About his meeting with Dr. Otto, Gurudev writes, “I was fortunate to meet Dr. Otto in Poona in 1927. I had read his book (The Idea of the Holy) and he also had read mine on the Upanishads, and he sent a note to me saying that he would come to see me. I replied, “Sir, you are very old. You need not come to me, but I will myself go to see you.” And so I went to him along with Dr. Macnicol and Dr. Belvalkar and had good discussion with him. He spoke English so well. And when I parted company with him, I said, “Sir, you are a real professor.”19

19- R. D. Ranade: The Bhagavadgita as a Philosophy of God Realisation (1959) Pg. 282
