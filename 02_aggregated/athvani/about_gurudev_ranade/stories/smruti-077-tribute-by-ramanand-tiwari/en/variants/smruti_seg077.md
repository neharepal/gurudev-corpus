76- Dr. Ramanand Tiwari

Head of Department of Philosophy, M.S.J. College, Bharatpur, Rajasthan

“Prof. Ranade was a legend in Allahabad, and outside in India also, for his philosophic acumen, his saintly life and his mystic faith. For ten years from 1938 I had the privilege of close association with him and of receiving divine spiritual inspiration from him. I was initiated into the Upanishadic through by him while I translated his ‘Constructive Survey of Upanishadic Philosophy’ into Hindi in 1938. I have preserved the personal copy of the original English book which he presented to me ‘as a small token of love’ under his own autograph.”92

“Gurudev Prof. R. D. Ranade was a mystic by faith and he was a mystic also in his life and conduct. He was ‘a phantom of divine delight’, an embodiment of spiritual inspiration. Gurudev Ranade was not a mere teacher of philosophy. He was not even a mere intellectual philosopher. He was a mystic who had realized the Truth. He was a living flame of philosophy. The enlightenment had culminated into a spark of experience. He had visualized the truth in his life, and it was transformed into a brilliant way of life. Prof. Ranade’s life was almost wholly spiritualized and he transmitted the spiritual effect to others spontaneously and unnoticed by others. His mysticism or spiritualism was creative in a remarkable and vital sense. Meditation for him was the practical way of faith, a way of God-realization.”93
