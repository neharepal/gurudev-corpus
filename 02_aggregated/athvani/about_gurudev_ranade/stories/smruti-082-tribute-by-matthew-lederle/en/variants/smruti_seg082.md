“Ramchandra Dattatraya Ranade, one of the dominant figures of the modern renaissance in India, made a new attempt to integrate these new forces, to bring to a synthesis the old and the new, the East and the West. When the introduction of works of Modern Indian authors into the curriculum of philosophy of Poona University was discussed, the names of those proposed, though in the end not accepted, were S. Radhakrishnan, Aurobindo Ghose and R. D. Ranade. The religious ideas of a number of educated contemporary Hindus resemble those proposed by Ranade. A knowledge of his thought, therefore, is an invaluable aid to an understanding of the religious climate in India. Ranade’s whole life was a sincere quest for truth; he was a witness of the desire in man to search for God.”100
