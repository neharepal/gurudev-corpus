80- Dr. Indu Prakash Singh

A Philosopher, Economist, Politician, Scholar and Author

Indian Ambassador to Nepal, Spain and Sudan

“In 1951, a fellow student of Philosophy Department in Allahabad University informed me that Prof. R. D. Ranade was going to give a talk on “Mysticism in Hindi Literature,”later in the evening. The desire to meet Prof. Ranade was so powerful that I made it a point to attend the lecture. That evening the speaker explained the significance of Meera Bai’s famous Bhajan- ‘जोगी मत जा मत जा |’ His deep analysis, given in clear and simple words, in which a lot of meaning given by the expression in upon his face rather than words, made me feel for the first time in my life that I was face to face with a sage, who deserved to be in line of the seers whom this country has produced since times immemorial.

Thereafter, through a close friend of mine I reached his place and then did not miss any excuse for going to his house. In the evenings, his drawing room used to be full of high dignitaries- senior Government officials, Judges of High Courts, Ministers of States and of Central Government or big political leaders. The thing which impressed me most in these gatherings at his house was the absence of any forbidding sense of aloofness or artificial mystery which people of lesser spiritual merit in this country tend to create around themselves. Gurudev as the host in his drawing room was completely different from what he was in his private room. His was an indescribable presence which caused an over-powering sensation on those who were around. During his silence, one got the impression as if he was communicating.

The most striking feature of Gurudev’s personality was his ability to talk and to think at the level of the person with whom he was at any time. It was an exhilarating experience to see the great mystic discussing the grossest problems of the people around him.

Our country’s greatest wealth has been the unbroken chain of our saints and seers. Gurudev Ranade carried his spiritual greatness in an invisible reservoir. The man in him was never sidetracked by the Sage.”98

“I have seen ‘All’ in one person and that is Dr. Ranade. I was impressed by the man in the saint than the saint in the man.”99
