20- Mahamahopadhyaya Dr. Sir Ganganath JhaVice-Chancellor, University of Allahabad

The Eminent Sanskrit Scholar and Thinker

Corresponding Fellow of the British Academy

Dr. Ganganath Jha, the then Vice-Chancellor of University of Allahabad wanted Prof. R. D. Ranade (Gurudev) as a successor to Dr. H. N. Randle in the Philosophy Department. He had known the remarkable contribution of Prof. Ranade to Greek Philosophy and Indian Philosophy. He also knew that Prof. Ranade was a renowned teacher. He was well acquainted with the Academy of Philosophy and Religion established by Prof. Ranade. The Academy was an eloquent proof of his organizing ability. Again the encyclopaedic History of Indian Philosophy which he had launched had marked him out as a scholar of versatility. He was also impressed by the profound impressions created by the book ‘The Constructive Survey of Upanishadic Philosophy’ world over. Dr. Ganganath Jha, therefore, started correspondence with Prof. Ranade early in 1927 with a view to bringing him into the portals of the University. Prof. Ranade (Gurudev) was reluctant to go to Allahabad as he was apprehensive about the extreme climate of the place as also about the managing the work of the Academy of Philosophy and Religion, Poona from there.23

Dr. Ganganath Jha allayed the fears of Gurudev in his letter dt. 1st October 1927 to him and conveyed his earnestness to have him (Gurudev) on the rolls of the University. The letter is as under:

“…..As any physical contact between yourself and the Academy office will not be necessary, I do not see why your work for the Academy should suffer in any way. As regards climate, Allahabad is very much drier than Poona. In fact the temperature reaches 1150F during summer.

Any way you can give us trial for a year, after that, if you do not find the conditions suitable, you can tell me so, and I shall see that the final step towards confirmation is not taken and you are relieved.

Please let me know by return post if I have your consent to put up your name before the committee that selects the professor.

I need not add that personally I shall regard it as my greatest achievement as Vice-Chancellor, if I succeed in bringing to the University a Professor of your calibre and attainments. So please don’t disappoint.”24

Finally, Dr. Jha wrote to Gurudev, “It does not matter even if you don't deliver a single lecture. I want to enter your name in the List of professors of our University.”25

Thereafter, in the Executive Council meeting held on 24 October 1927 a resolution was passed that Prof. R. D. Ranade be appointed Professor of Philosophy for a period of three years in the first instance.

After this final request, Shri Gurudev joined the Allahabad University as a Professor and Head of the Department of Philosophy on 1st December 1927.
